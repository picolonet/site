About

We live in a different reality today. People who use tech products on their smartphones are aware of how they are being sold as products to ad networks. Every experience is designed to “monetize” their time. As users, we have lost control of our own data to a small number of powerful tech platforms.


At the same time, the amount of distrust on the information on the Web has grown significantly. People have become increasingly distrustful of intermediaries such as media portals, content platforms, social and other networks. Web3 has the promise to solve both problems using a decentralized network of computers to provide the compute and data services in order to run apps. Blockchain and token-based systems allow for much better incentivization around content creation and distribution rather than the single method of monetizing the user’s time.


Our mission at Picolo Labs is to build the infrastructure to support such applications. Now is the time to re-invent the key concepts around compute and data to work in a decentralized world. How should a fully decentralized database running over a 1000 nodes work ? How should these nodes talk to each other ? What if some of these nodes are malicious ? These are the problems we work on every day at Picolo Labs. Our founding team consists of two ex-Google engineers who have solid backgrounds in building scalable systems that serve billions of users every single day.


Fun fact about the logo:

We chose the number PI (first two letters of Picolo) as it is irrational and thus infinite in representation. That is, there is no way to represent PI in a finite manner in the language of math we have today, much like most of the data in the world. The logo is constructed of 1s and 0s taken from the first 128 bits of the binary representation of PI.

Synopsis


Picolo is a well-funded pre-launch startup by two ex-Google engineers in SF Bay area. The startup is building a fully decentralized distributed data network to support web3 apps. The technology is disruptive to incumbent web2 apps while also supporting applications built for blockchains such as Ethereum. Checkout our whitepaper at https://picolo.network/paper.


If this sounds exciting to you, read on !


What you will be doing


  • Build a decentralized network of nodes storing shards of data to be shared in a peer to peer network.
  • Design decentralized app SDKs and API's to handle sensitive and confidential data.
  • Design, architect, develop, and implement a premier decentralized network on blockchain that allows for strong data security, excellent control of data, ease of integration, reduction of cost and creation of new profit centers.
  • Integrate with apps built on Ethereum with Solidity / smart contracts, also work with Golang, Python and ReactJS.
  • Craft a decentralized solution to a typically centralized problem from the ground up.
  • Develop models within distributed computing frameworks.
  • Maintain a well-tested codebase with continuous integration.

What you need for this position

  • Experience in Go, building backends on Google Cloud/AWS/Azure
  • Experience with continuous integration tools, git, test driven development. Experience launching backends is a plus.
  • Basic understanding of networking concepts, distributed systems and basic concepts in databases such as SQL, NoSql.
  • Experience with Ethereum is a plus.
